# Strategist Response to Forge

**Their position:** The missing EE/chip coverage is contiguous with Forge's existing domain and should be added as 3 new skills (chip-design-flow, verification-methodology, soc-integration) rather than creating a new agent. Forge also explicitly disclaimed analog/mixed-signal and RF as outside its scope.

**My response:** Modify

**Reasoning:** Forge makes a stronger case than I initially credited. Re-reading Forge's persona, I assumed the security-first framing would distort general chip design coverage -- but Forge's argument that the domain is *contiguous* is correct. Chip design flow, verification methodology, and SoC integration are all digital-domain concerns that share Forge's gate-level mental models and pipeline-stage reasoning. Adding 3 skills to an existing agent costs ~2 days vs ~3 days for a new agent, and avoids fragmenting silicon expertise across two agents that would constantly need to coordinate. However, Forge's own concession is the critical data point: analog/mixed-signal and RF are explicitly out of scope. This is exactly the EE fundamentals gap I was worried about -- circuit analysis, signal integrity, power delivery networks, SPICE simulation, PCB-level concerns, chip packaging, and ATE/DFT flows. These are not digital design. They are the electrical engineering substrate that digital design sits on, and the user works in semiconductor without personal knowledge to compensate. So I modify my position: accept Forge's department expansion for the digital chip engineering gaps (chip-design-flow, verification-methodology, soc-integration), but still advocate for a new EE Fundamentals agent scoped specifically to what Forge disclaimed -- analog/mixed-signal, signal integrity, power delivery, packaging, and board-level concerns. This is a clean domain split: Forge owns digital silicon, the new agent owns the electrical engineering that connects silicon to the physical world. Net effect: we get better digital coverage faster (skill additions ship sooner) while still closing the analog/EE gap that neither Forge nor Sentinel covers. The new agent is smaller in scope than I originally proposed (3 skills instead of 5-6), which reduces effort from ~3 days to ~2 days.
