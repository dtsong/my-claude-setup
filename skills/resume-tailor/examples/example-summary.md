# Example: Scripps Health tailoring summary

> Scope: This example demonstrates the tailoring pattern for a non-retail JD (healthcare). It preserves Alan's voice (his preferred verbs, his summary tone, his title framing) while adjusting the subtitle, summary specialty, and bullet order to surface JD-relevant work.

## Fit analysis

- **Direct hits:** Multi-site experience (51 stores), zero-downtime upgrades, ITSM build with measurable reduction, QA/test automation, SQL fluency, vendor management with contract negotiation.
- **Adjacent:** Hermès is not healthcare, but multi-site governance, reliability discipline, and regulated-context decision-making (multi-country tax, audit posture) translate. The Michaels Companies PM work (1,200+ stores) reinforces multi-site PM capability.
- **Gaps:** No direct healthcare IT experience. No EHR (Epic, Cerner) exposure. No HIPAA-specific compliance role. No cloud architecture experience (AWS dropped from skills). Surface these honestly in cover letter and interview rather than papering over.

## What was changed

1. **Subtitle.** "Retail Systems Manager · Omnichannel Integration · Retail" → "Retail Systems Manager · Multi-Site Operations · Enterprise Integrations." Drops the second "Retail" emphasis; surfaces the angles Scripps explicitly asks for. Kept "Retail Systems Manager" as the lead descriptor since that's Alan's authoritative positioning.

2. **Summary.** Same opening sentence as base (Alan's voice preserved). Inserted "multi-site platform upgrades with no deployment downtime" into the specialty list. Added "under regulated conditions" to the closing line. No verb upgrades; kept "managing," "delivering," etc.

3. **Expertise lines.** "Retail Systems Management" → "Enterprise Systems Management" in line 1. Reordered to surface "ITSM & SLA Governance" and "Contract Negotiation" prominently.

4. **Hermès bullet order reordered:**
   - #1: Zero-downtime upgrade bullet (direct JD match: "minimal disruption")
   - #2: ITSM build (direct JD match)
   - #3: Ranorex/QA bullet (direct JD match: "QA and testing practices")
   - #4: Cegid–Salesforce integration
   - #5: Omnichannel integration
   - #6: SQL troubleshooting
   - #7: AI tooling (JD lists AI/ML as "nice to have")

5. **Light bullet rephrasing.** Added "with no disruption to operational users" to the upgrades bullet and "reducing regression risk on platform releases" to the Ranorex bullet, both of which connect Alan's actual work to specific JD concerns. No new claims introduced.

6. **Earlier roles.** Light tightening (dropped one weak Barneys bullet for length). Michaels bullets trimmed to 4 from 5 (the vague "project administration" bullet dropped).

7. **Skills section.** "Retail Platforms" → "Enterprise Platforms"; added "ITSM frameworks" to IT Operations; "cloud-hosted retail systems" → "cloud-hosted enterprise systems."

## What was NOT changed

- All dates, employer names, role titles preserved exactly
- No metrics, technologies, or accomplishments invented
- Alan's preferred verbs ("managed," "led," "adopted," "delivered") preserved throughout
- No em-dashes added (zero em-dashes in final output)
- Healthcare experience NOT fabricated; gaps flagged in fit analysis

## Cover letter angle

Lead with: Scripps is a multi-site healthcare network where reliability is mission-critical. Alan has spent 7+ years delivering multi-country platform upgrades with no deployment downtime in a regulated industry (luxury retail also has regulatory constraints: multi-country tax compliance, audit posture). The transfer is real and worth making explicit.

Mention: based in San Diego, available for on-site interviews.

Be honest about: no direct healthcare IT background. Frame as "the systems discipline transfers; the domain is learnable." Do not overclaim healthcare familiarity.

Keep em-dashes to zero. Use Alan's measured tone, not aspirational language.
